U.S. Department of Energy

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) was established in 1977 and is responsible for overseeing the country’s energy production, distribution, and consumption. The DOE's mission is to ensure America’s security and prosperity by addressing its energy, environmental, and nuclear challenges.

Key Responsibilities

  • Manage the nation’s nuclear weapons program
  • Promote energy conservation and efficiency
  • Support scientific research in energy technologies

Notable Programs

The DOE administers several programs focused on renewable energy, including the Solar Energy Technologies Office and the Wind Energy Technologies Office.

Recent Developments

In 2023, the DOE announced plans to increase funding for clean energy initiatives to reduce carbon emissions by 50% by 2030.

U.S. Department of the Treasury

The U.S. Department of the Treasury was established in 1789 and is responsible for managing government revenue and economic policy. The Treasury’s mission is to maintain a strong economy and create economic and job opportunities by promoting conditions that enable economic growth.

Key Responsibilities

  • Oversee the federal budget
  • Manage government debt and revenues
  • Enforce economic sanctions

Notable Programs

The Treasury oversees the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) and various financial crime enforcement programs.

Recent Developments

In 2023, the Treasury introduced new measures to enhance financial transparency and combat money laundering.
